MS Dhoni [THALA]

Celebrating the Legend

Biography

Ranchi boy to cricketing god, Dhoni's tale is etched in glory's code. Unorthodox flair, lightning reflexes, he rose like a comet, defying all complexes. Captain Cool, ice under fire, his sixes sent bowlers into pyre. World Cups conquered, nerves calmed with the "helicopter," a legend sculpted, a billion souls set a-glitter. Beyond the boundary, an idol took flight, proving dreams soar beyond mere might. Though retired, his legacy rings true, Captain Cool, forever in you.


As Mahendra Singh Dhoni announces his retirement from international cricket, we pay tribute to the cricketing legend and a true inspiration to millions.

ACHIEVEMENTS
  • World Cup Champion (2011): Leading India to a thrilling victory on home soil against Sri Lanka in the 2011 World Cup final cemented Dhoni's place as a legend. His unbeaten 91* under pressure was a masterclass in finishing.
  • Champions Trophy Champion (2013): Dhoni became the first captain to win all three major ICC trophies (World Cup, Champions Trophy, and T20 World Cup) when India triumphed in England in 2013.
  • Highest Score by a Wicket-keeper in ODIs: Dhoni's unbeaten 183 against Sri Lanka in 2013 remains the highest score by a wicket-keeper in ODIs.
  • Most Sixes as Captain: Dhoni holds the record for most sixes hit as a captain in international cricket, showcasing his power hitting ability.
  • Most Stumpings in International Cricket: Dhoni's lightning-fast reflexes and anticipation behind the stumps have resulted in him taking the most stumpings in international cricket history.
  • ** acrobatic catches:** Dhoni has taken some of the most spectacular catches in cricket history, leaving fans in awe of his athleticism and skill.
  • Padma Bhushan and Padma Shri Awards: Dhoni's contribution to Indian cricket has been recognized with India's highest civilian awards, the Padma Bhushan and Padma Shri.
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year (2008 & 2009): Dhoni's exceptional performances earned him the coveted ICC ODI Player of the Year award twice.
  • Chennai Super Kings Captain (2008-Present): Dhoni has led the Chennai Super Kings to four IPL titles, making him one of the most successful captains in the tournament's history.
